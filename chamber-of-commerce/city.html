City

Introduction Video

Toamasina, meaning "like salt" or "salty", unofficially and in French Tamatave, is the capital of the Atsinanana region on the east coast of Madagascar on the Indian Ocean. The city is the chief seaport of the country, situated 215 km (134 mi) northeast of its capital and biggest city Antananarivo. In 2018 Toamasina had a population of 325,857. (From wikipedia)

History

Under French rule, Toamasina was the seat of several foreign consuls, as well as of numerous French officials, and was the chief port for the capital and the interior. Imports consisted principally of piece-goods, farinaceous foods, and iron and steel goods; main exports were gold dust, raffia, hides, caoutchouc (rubber) and live animals. During the colonial period, owing to the character of the soil and the formerly crowded native population, the town was often plagued by epidemics: the plague broke out in 1898, and again in 1900; but since the draining of the neighboring marshes, there was an improvement. After 1895, the native population was removed from the town and settled in a new village to the north-west

Demographic

Toamasina is a cosmopolitan city, mainly populated by the betsimisaraka. In addition to the other ethnic groups on the island, it is also home to a very large Chinese (most often mixed), Indo-Pakistani community, as well as a European minority. With a population of over 200,000 inhabitants (2005 estimates), it is the second largest agglomeration in Madagascar, behind Antananarivo the capital, but ahead of Antsirabe and Fianarantsoa.

Climate

Toamasina features a trade-wind tropical rainforest climate under the Köppen climate classification. While Toamasina has no true dry season month where less than 60 millimetres (2.4 inches) of precipitation (on average) falls, the seaport has noticeably wetter and drier periods of the year. September–November is the driest period of the year, while February–April is the wettest time of the year. Average temperatures are relatively constant throughout the course of the year, though it is slightly cooler in the months of July and August, where average highs are around 24 °C (75 °F), and warmer in the months of January and February, where high temperatures on average are 30 °C (86 °F). Toamasina averages roughly 3,368.2 millimetres (132.61 inches) of precipitation annually.

TOAMASINA, THE MULTI-FACETTED CITY

Keeping vestiges from before independence, Tamatave has many must-see tourist sites. The old Tamatave, the Place Bien Aimé, the port of Tamatave or the north coast and its idyllic beaches are all places to explore in this colorful city. You will enjoy these varied decorations, aboard a rickshaw, a quad or even lying under a parasol.
